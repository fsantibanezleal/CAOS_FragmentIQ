# Attribution

## Methods

FragmentIQ implements established blast-fragmentation PSD + image-based granulometry. The methods are attributable to:

- **Rosin, P. & Rammler, E. (1933).** The laws governing the fineness of powdered coal. *Journal of the Institute of
  Fuel*, 7, 29–36., the Rosin–Rammler distribution fitted to the recovered PSD.
- **Cunningham, C. V. B. (1983).** The Kuz-Ram model for prediction of fragmentation from blasting. *Proc. 1st Int.
  Symp. on Rock Fragmentation by Blasting*, 439–453., fragmentation summarised as (xc, n).
- **Ouchterlony, F. (2005).** The Swebrec© function: linking fragmentation by blasting and crushing. *Mining
  Technology*, 114(1), 29–44., the fines extension of the RR curve.
- **Vincent, L. & Soille, P. (1991).** Watersheds in digital spaces: an efficient algorithm based on immersion
  simulations. *IEEE T-PAMI*, 13(6), 583–598., the marker-controlled watershed delineation.
- **Maerz, N. H., Palangio, T. C. & Franklin, J. A. (1996).** WipFrag image based granulometry system. *Proc.
  FRAGBLAST 5*, 91–99., the image-granulometry workflow FragmentIQ mirrors.
- **Sanchidrián, J. A., Segarra, P., Ouchterlony, F. & López, L. M. (2009).** On the accuracy of fragment size
  measurement by image analysis. *Rock Mechanics and Rock Engineering*, 42, 95–116., the over-segmentation /
  delineation-bias literature FragmentIQ is honest about.

Full bibliography in `frontend/src/data/citations.ts` and the in-app Methodology page.

Added in 0.08.000:

- **Otsu, N. (1979).** A threshold selection method from gray-level histograms. *IEEE T-SMC*, 9(1), 62-66.,
  the adaptive foreground threshold for real photos.
- **Serra, J. (1982).** *Image Analysis and Mathematical Morphology*. Academic Press (after Matheron 1975).,
  the morphological granulometry (opening-by-size) used as the delineation-free reference.
- **Ronneberger, O., Fischer, P. & Brox, T. (2015).** U-Net. *MICCAI*., and **Kirillov, A. et al. (2023).**
  Segment Anything. *ICCV*., the deep-segmentation SOTA cited as an offline reference.
- **Zhao, J., Li, D. & Yu, Y. (2024).** Identification of Rock Fragments after Blasting by Using SAM.
  *Minerals*, 14(7), 654., the domain application of SAM.

## Data

The synthetic muckpile images are **synthetic**, rock fragments sized by a Rosin–Rammler distribution, placed by
non-overlap tiling on dark inter-fragment gaps, radially shaded, generated by `frontend/src/frag/scene.ts`
(seeded). The `C-MONO` and `C-KNOWN` cases are closed-form analytic controls.

The **Real sample** lane redistributes a curated 5-image subset of real post-blast rock-fragment photographs:

- **Yaghoobi, H. (2018).** *fragmented rocks by blasting*. Mendeley Data, V1. doi:10.17632/z78ghz96bn.1.
  Licensed **CC BY 4.0**. 226 photographs from the Gole-Gohar iron ore mine, Iran. The bundled subset
  (`data/derived/real/FR-01..FR-05.jpg`, downscaled) is redistributed under CC BY 4.0 with this attribution.
  There is NO sieve-measured PSD ground truth in the source; the red scale ball physical diameter is
  undocumented, so FragmentIQ reports RELATIVE, pixel-relative estimates only. Images were obtained via the
  Apache-2.0 mirror `github.com/Yairama/SAM-Rock-Fragmentation`, which cites the same Mendeley origin.
